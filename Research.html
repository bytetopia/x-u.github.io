What Makes Geeks Tick? A Study of Stack Overflow Careers, with Tingting Nian (NYU Stern) and Luis Cabral (NYU Stern)

Online communities such as Stack Overflow provide an ideal setting for studying incentives on voluntary contribution to public goods. One particularly intriguing question is, to what extent, such contribution is driven by career concerns. We estimate the magnitude of the impact of career concerns on contribution to Stack Overflow using a set of users who experience a change in job seeking status, a proxy for a change in career concerns. Contributors who change their job seeking status from active to passive, on average, experience a 43% drop in activity, measured as the number of answers provided in a month. By exploring plausible mechanisms that might explain such changes in contribution level such as time constraints and controlling for individual characteristics, our results demonstrate robustness across different model specifications.
